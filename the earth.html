THE EARTH

➢ Earth – The Blue Planet :

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and is about 4.54 billion years old, with a diameter of approximately 12,742 km. About 71% of Earth’s surface is covered by water, including oceans, rivers, lakes, and glaciers, while 29% consists of land such as continents, mountains, plains, and deserts. Earth’s atmosphere is mainly made up of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), along with small amounts of other gases. This atmosphere protects living things from harmful solar radiation, helps control temperature, and creates weather and climate patterns. The Earth has three main layers: the crust, which is the thin outer layer containing land and ocean basins; the mantle, a thick semi-solid layer responsible for plate tectonics; and the core, which includes a solid inner core and a liquid outer core that produces Earth’s magnetic field. The Earth rotates once every 24 hours, causing day and night, and revolves around the Sun in about 365.25 days, creating seasons because of its 23.5° tilted axis. Earth contains abundant liquid water, which is essential for life, and it is the only known planet that supports diverse ecosystems and millions of species, including humans. The planet’s surface is constantly changing due to volcanic eruptions, earthquakes, erosion, and plate tectonics. Because of its water, protective atmosphere, suitable temperature, and stable environment, Earth is uniquely capable of supporting life and is often called the “Blue Planet.”

➠ Earth Structure

➢ Prominent

Earth like a solid ball consist of many ‘onion like’ layers. The three main layers are the core, the mantle and the crust. The core is the inner most part of the earth, the crust is the outer part and between them is the mantle. The earth is surrounded by the atmosphere.

➢ The Core

The inner most part of the earth is the core. For the observation of which no direct evidence is yet recorded. This part of the earth is about 2,900 km (1,800 miles) below the earth’s surface. The core is a dense ball of the elements iron and nickel, it is divided into two parts (two inner core and outer core). The outer core is miles 1,250 km thick. even though temperatures reach 6700°F (3700°C). The outer core is so great that it cannot melt. Because the earth rotates, the outer core spins around the inner core and that causes the earth’s magnetism.
(Source: Kondo and Taniguchi (2005))

➢ The Mantle

The layer above the core is called the mantle. It begins generally about 6 miles (10 km) below the oceanic crust and about 19 miles (30 km) below the continental crust (The crust). The mantle is to divide into the inner mantle and the outer mantle. It is about 1,800 miles (2,900 km) thick and makes up nearly 80 percent of the Earth’s total volume.

➢ The Crust

The crust is the most exposed layer of the earth. The crust lies above the mantle. It is the earth’s hard outer shell, the surface on which we are living. In relation with the other layers the crust is much thinner. It floats upon the softer, denser mantle. The crust is made up of solid materials but these materials are not everywhere the same. There is an Oceanic crust and a Continental crust. The first one is about 4.7 miles (6-11 km) thick and consists of heavy rocks, like basalt. The Continental crust is thicker than the Oceanic crust, about 19 miles (30 km) thick. It is mainly made up of light material, like granite.

➢ The Atmosphere

The earth is surrounded by various kind of gases. This composition of gas is called the earth’s atmosphere. The atmosphere is made up of different layers with different qualities. It consists of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, 0.93% carbon dioxide and 0.04% of other gases. The Troposphere is the layer where the weather happens, above this layer within the Stratosphere is the ozone layer that absorbs the Sun’s harmful ultraviolet rays. Above the Stratosphere is the Mesosphere, the ozone layer that in which the Ionosphere and the Exosphere. The atmosphere is about 500 miles (800 km) thick.
